Basis convergence

Among the advantages of the use of range separation methods, one expects the convergence of the calculations with respect to the size of the gaussian basis set to be faster since the long-range wavefunction is freed from the treatment of the electron-electron coalescence. Indeed, we showed, on the one hand, the exponential convergence of the quality of the expansion called “partial-wave” of the long-range wavefunction at the electron-electron coalescence with respect to the angular momentum of the expansion and, on the other hand, the also exponential convergence of the error made on the total RSH+MP2 energy with respect to the cardinal number of the Dunning basis. Based on these observations, we proposed an extrapolation method for the total energy of range-separated calculations.

Basis convergence of range-separated density-functional theory.
O. Franck, B. Mussard, E. Luppi, J. Toulouse, J. Chem. Phys. 142 074107 (2015)
