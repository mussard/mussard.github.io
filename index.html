My research consists in developments in electronic structure of molecular and solid systems. There are two fundamental aspects to my work. On the one hand, my approach is analytic and theoretical and consists in developping new methods and approximations. On the other hand, an integral part of my work is technical and is about implementing these developments in widely used quantum chemistry packages.

A part of my part focuses in the description of London dispersion forces in Density Functional Theory (DFT) in the context of range-separated theories coupled with the Random Phase Approximation (RPA).

I have more recently been working in Quantum Monte-Carlo (QMC) and implementing in the CHAMP software.

I currently am working on implementations of efficient multi-reference perturbation theory (MRPT) for applications to systems of interest in biological processes and energy conversion and storage.
